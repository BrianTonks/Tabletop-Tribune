Is D&D Entering the "Daggerheart Era"?

The Curator's Take: For nearly a decade, the names Jeremy Crawford and Chris Perkins were synonymous with Dungeons & Dragons. Now, they are the creative engines behind Darrington Press (Critical Role's publishing arm). This isn't just a job change; it's a massive shift in the tectonic plates of the TTRPG industry. Here is the timeline of how we got here, and what it means for your table in 2026.

Jan 31, 2019

The Legend of Spurt the Kobold

Long before teaming up at Darrington Press, Chris Perkins gave the Critical Role community one of its most legendary guest appearances in Campaign 2, Episode 50 ("The Endless Burrows"). Playing a Kobold Inventor named Spurt, Perkins survived a grand total of 15 minutes.

"How long do Kobolds live?"
"ELEVEN DAYS!" — Spurt, right before being instantly squashed by a Fire Giant.

Why it matters: It showcased the undeniable chemistry between the D&D design team and the Critical Role cast, laying the cultural groundwork for their future partnership.

Read the full Episode Recap
June 16, 2025

The Industry Shocker: The Move to Darrington Press

The TTRPG world shook when it was announced that both Jeremy Crawford and Chris Perkins were departing Wizards of the Coast. They officially joined Darrington Press as Game Director and Creative Director, respectively, bringing decades of 5e design experience to Critical Role's proprietary systems.

Read the original announcement
March 2026

Acquisitions Incorporated & The Daggerheart Era

Crawford officially signaled the changing of the guard by announcing that the long-running live-play series Acquisitions Incorporated would be transitioning from D&D to the Daggerheart system. In recent interviews, Crawford has teased how their design philosophy is evolving past the d20 system into Daggerheart's "Hope and Fear" mechanics.
